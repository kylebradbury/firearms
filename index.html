Guns and the United States

The United States is an outlier in its relationship with gun ownership when compared to the global community. The U.S. has the highest rate of gun ownership in the world at 101 civilian guns per 100 people, more guns than people. In fact, the countries with the next highest gun ownership rate have half as many guns per 100 people (55 per 100 people in Yemen and 46 in Switzerland). Additionally, the gun homicide rate of the U.S. is higher than comparable countries in terms of per capita GDP.
